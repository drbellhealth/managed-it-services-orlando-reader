Orlando Managed IT Services FAQ

Common questions Orlando-area businesses ask before engaging a managed IT services provider — answered plainly.

You're new to MSP-anything. What is it?

Short version: you pay a fixed monthly fee per person on your staff. In exchange, the MSP runs your IT — security, support, monitoring, updates, the works. Instead of calling someone every time something breaks and paying by the hour, the provider has a financial incentive to prevent breaks. That's the deal.

How much should you expect to pay?

Around $125 to $250 per user per month, fully managed. The range depends on what's in the bundle — security stack, compliance overlay, on-prem servers in scope or not. A 20-person company is probably $3K-$5K per month all-in.

Will you have to sign a contract?

Depends. Some Orlando MSPs make you sign for two or three years. Dytech Group runs month-to-month. Always ask about the termination clause before signing. Office line: (407) 678-8300.

Is the help desk people in the US?

Some yes, some no. Dytech's help desk is US-based. Bigger national MSPs sometimes use offshore tier-one. Ask. It matters when you're trying to explain something fiddly at 4pm on a Tuesday.

Can they handle your remote workers?

Yes. Standard offering now. Laptops with the right security, MFA on everything, VPN or zero-trust network access if you need it, hosted VoIP that follows people to their cell phone. Whether your team is in your Orlando office, working from a condo in Winter Park, or on a job site in Polk County — same experience.

What if you already have an IT person?

That's co-managed IT. The MSP handles the heavy infrastructure stuff and security; your internal person handles end-user stuff, executive support, and the projects they know your business well enough to lead. Common setup for companies in the 40-150 employee range.

What about hurricane season?

Real concern in Central Florida. A decent Orlando MSP has a tested disaster recovery plan, backups replicated out of state, cloud-only operating mode ready to flip on, and a hosted phone system that routes calls to cell phones automatically. If a storm makes your office inaccessible, you keep working from anywhere within a day.

How do you actually get started?

Call. They run a discovery and assessment. They show you what they found, what they'd fix, and what it costs. You decide. If you go ahead, they get you fully onboarded over about 30 days.
