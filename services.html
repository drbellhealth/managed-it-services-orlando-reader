Managed Services & Co-Managed IT

Managed services means you pay a fixed monthly fee per user. The MSP takes over your IT. Monitoring, patching, security, help desk, projects. No more hourly bills when something breaks. The provider has skin in the game on preventing breaks. That's the whole pitch.

US-Based Help Desk & End-User Support

Help desk is where your staff calls when something doesn't work. Dytech's help desk is in the US. Real technicians who understand Orlando. Some big national MSPs use offshore. Ask before signing — it matters when you're frustrated and trying to explain a problem.

Cybersecurity, EDR & SOC Coverage

Security is the biggest reason most companies finally call an MSP. Ransomware, phishing, wire fraud. The MSP runs the layered stack: MFA on everything, EDR on every endpoint, email security, training, backups. Most attacks fail somewhere in the layers. The ones that get through become manageable incidents instead of business-ending events.

Cloud, Microsoft 365 & VoIP

Cloud means Microsoft 365 — email, OneDrive, SharePoint, Teams. The MSP admins your tenant, fixes the license sprawl, secures the configuration. VoIP means hosted business phones — no more PBX in a closet. Calls follow you to your cell during hurricanes. Both are commodity. Integration with the rest of your environment is what matters.

What Onboarding Looks Like

First thirty days. Week 1: they look at everything you have. Week 2: they install monitoring and fix the obvious gaps. Week 3: your staff starts calling them instead of whoever you used to call. Week 4: things are running. Then the bigger projects (security improvements, equipment refreshes, the strategic stuff) get sequenced over the next few months.
